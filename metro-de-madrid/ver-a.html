Madrid de Metro

Oct. 10, 2023  |  Genevive Sanchez

Intro

Madrid, one of the world's major global cities, is the capital city of Spain. Not only that, it is often dubbed as having one of Europe's most extensive metro systems. It came into existence in 1919 has has had a lot of successful expansions.


Garnered Popularity

Though its development and infrastructure is similar to its counterparts, the strong ridership of Metro de Madrid comes from its extensive mobility and safety measures. It consists of subway and light rail tram lines that do not strictly run in the north-south or east-west direction; some lines run in both or even have a circular route. Moreover, the lines provide access to four major train stations, the Barajas Airport to the northwest, interchanges as well as every major business and shopping district. Metro stations are often a 10-minute walk away from a commuter's location — if a commuter isn't at a large public park such as the Casa de Campo or Parque del Buen Retiro. This allows commuters to make their necessary trips around the city without having to worry about a route to get to the metro station itself.


With regards to its safety measures, passengers are required to be in possession of a validated ticket before getting admittance into the turnstiles and station platforms. Passengers can get a fine of 80€ for being caught without a ticket. Secondly, private security is posted at each train station, many of whom take intermittent rounds on the trains.
